EXCERPT FROM THE ANCIENT TOME

  Long before the castle library had shelves, the scribes of Linuxia
  kept secrets in small files tucked beside ordinary records.

  The ancient scribes could view invisible entries by invoking ls -a,
  the sacred "show all" incantation.

  Files that begin with a . are hidden from normal view. The scribes
  used this to keep drafts, notes, and experimental spells from cluttering
  the daily ledgers.

  A hidden file is not gone; it is merely omitted from the ordinary list.
  This is why careful scholars sometimes look with ls -a before declaring
  a room empty.

  Merlin warns that secrets are still files. They can be read, changed,
  copied, and removed when permissions allow it.

  The wisest archivists keep their names clear, their paths tidy, and
  their notes where future travelers can discover them.
